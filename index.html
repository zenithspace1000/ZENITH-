Our story

Started in a small computer centre in Khanakul

Maxmin Foundation grew out of a small computer training centre run by Max and Min in Khanakul, Hooghly. Over time, it became clear that many capable students from local government schools simply couldn't access — or afford — basic computer education, let alone newer fields like AI and Machine Learning.

We're formalising that work as an NGO: running structured courses, setting aside scholarship seats for students who can't pay, and partnering with local schools so that digital literacy isn't limited to those who can afford private coaching.

Our approach stays practical and local — small batches, hands-on systems, and a curriculum that mixes everyday digital skills with an honest, age-appropriate introduction to where computing is heading.

আমরা শুধু কম্পিউটার শেখাই না — আমরা সুযোগ তৈরি করি।

The problem we're solving

Why this matters in Khanakul

Government schools in our area often don't have a working computer lab or a teacher trained in IT — and the private institutes that do offer these courses are priced well beyond what most families here can pay.

Limited computer labs in government schools

Many students get little or no hands-on time with a computer during school hours.

Private institutes are out of reach

Course fees at private centres are often more than a family's monthly income — putting them out of reach entirely.

The skills gap keeps widening

Every year without exposure to current technology widens the gap between rural and urban students entering the job market.

Our response

Structured, bilingual, hands-on computer education — free for students who need it, taught by mentors who live in the community.

Digital literacy

More than just learning to type

টাইপিং শেখার চেয়ে অনেক বেশি

Being digitally literate in 2026 means knowing how to navigate the internet safely, protect yourself from scams, make digital payments, and use government services online. These are skills that affect everyday life — not just a future career.

Every Maxmin Foundation course weaves in digital literacy as a foundation: online safety, how to spot fake news, responsible use of AI tools, and basic cybersecurity habits that students can share with their families too.
